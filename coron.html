Let's Travel

Why Coron ?

Coron is a renowned beach and island vacation in Palawan. A lush tropical hideaway consists of the eastern part of Busuanga Island, Coron Island, and 50 other nearby islands that are all independent from the main Palawan island. Many people are drawn to it because of its World War II wreck diving locations, magnificent rock formations, and crystal blue seas. It is often regarded as the more tranquil and relaxed brother of El Nido, another popular Palawan vacation because to its beautiful seas and environment. Coron has a certain appeal that should be added to your travel bucket list when visiting the Philippines.

Kayangan Lake

Kayangan Lake is the most popular attraction on Coron Island, and with good reason. You enter the 'parking lot,' which is a pristine reef with crystal clear water. After leaving the boat, go up the stairs to the overlook before descending the opposite side to reach the famed Kayangan Lake. It's a freshwater lake with spectacular underwater rock formations. It's undoubtedly one of my favorite free-dive locations on the planet. Make your way to the left side of the lake for the best free-dive spot.

Twin Lagoon

Twin Lagoon is a beautiful pool of water hidden among the gorgeous rocks. Swim beneath the cliff and into Twin Lagoon through a little crevice. Once within the lagoon, you are encircled by towering, dagger-like cliffs in all directions while you float away from your problems in a private swimming pool.






Mt. Tapyas

Coron boasts a 742-step walk to the peak of Mt. Tapyas, a Hollywood-style 'CORON' sign sitting on the hill below a big cross, and a half-day town tour for under $10 USD. The climb will only take around 10-15 minutes, and the view is well worth it. The harbor, hundreds of residences constructed barely a few feet above the sea, and a spectacular view of the ocean and neighboring islands can all be seen from the top. The rear side of the viewpoint, where undulating hills transported the tourist to a distant area, was the highlight of the trek.
